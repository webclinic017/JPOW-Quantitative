Retail Sentiment

Track daily retail sentiment against the performance of the S&P500

Commission free trading has made stock trading extremely popular among everyday persons. One of the most popular trading platforms, Robinhood, has over 13M users. This page allows you to track the market sentiment of retail traders through a popular investing forum. Everyday, comments are scraped and run through a one vs rest multinomial naive bayes classification model. The text classifer labels comments as bullish, bearish, or neutral. The ratio of bullish comments to bearish comments is displayed as a gauge chart (indicating positive sentiment). The data is stored in a database so you can compare the forum sentiment to the price of the S&P500. The stock price is lagging compared to the sentiment, therefore, you can see if the sentiment was in the right or wrong direction after-the-fact and use the current forum sentiment to predict the movement of the S&P500 stock price.

Insider Trading

See what moves our senators are making

The Stop Trading on Congressional Knowledge (STOCK) Act requires senators to report financial transactions. Data is published at https://efdsearch.senate.gov/search/home/. Unfortunately, transactions are not published in real-time. Bi-weekly, data is scraped from the site and into a table to show sales and/or purchases of stocks and their amounts.

CEO Compensation

Who is worth their money

Visualize the highest performing CEOs of over 2000 companies by their YTD stock return. The correlation coefficient is also displayed to measure the relationship between stock return and the ceo's salary.

Unusual Options Activity

Track unusual volume on options contracts of over 2000 stocks

Everyday, options data is scraped and parsed to find options where the volume is greater than the open interest by atleast a set threshold value.
